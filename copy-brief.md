## Copy Principles Summary

Five non-negotiable rules for all skinmap copy, now and in the future:

**1. The physician is the hero. skinmap is the co-pilot.**
Never position skinmap as the smart one. The physician is the expert — skinmap makes them faster, more confident, and more capable. Every headline, feature description, and CTA should reinforce physician authority, not replace it. Wrong: "AI diagnoses your skin." Right: "AI-backed confidence at every appointment."

**2. Lead with the number. Then translate it for humans.**
Physicians read clinical data; patients read outcomes. Use both — in that order. Present the precise statistic first ("90% sensitivity"), then give the human translation ("flags 9 in 10 suspicious lesions"). This respects the physician audience while making the claim legible to patients.

**3. "Clinical-grade" is skinmap's most important modifier.**
The direct competitor (SkinCheck) is a consumer app. skinmap's sharpest differentiator is that it is built for clinical workflows, validated in clinical studies, and used by physicians — not by patients scanning themselves in bathroom mirrors. The phrase "clinical-grade" should appear on every page.

**4. Urgency without alarm. Authority without jargon.**
Skin cancer copy can easily veer into fear-mongering. Avoid it. The right register is: confident, data-backed urgency. "The five-year survival rate for Stage I melanoma exceeds 98%. For Stage IV, it drops below 30%" is more effective than "skin cancer kills." Give physicians and patients facts — let the facts carry the emotional weight.

**5. Every patient section should end with a physician action.**
Patient copy exists to create bottom-up demand pressure on physicians. Always close patient-facing sections with a prompt that routes action through the physician: "Ask your doctor about skinmap" — not "Download the app." This preserves the physician-mediated distribution model and respects the clinical relationship.

## Copy Analysis: What the Best Reference Sites Do

From analysis of Sword Health, Vista AI, SkinCheck, Biobeat, Mainstay Medical, and Owlet:

- **Sword Health**: Leads with ROI (3.2:1 savings ratio) before outcomes — B2B buyer logic first, patient impact second. Their best move: quoting the exact NPS (83) and surgery reduction (50%) as standalone stat cards.
- **Vista AI**: "Taking MRI Scanning Beyond Human Limits" — a bold transformation claim using the word "limits." Leads with FDA clearance in the subheadline. Quotes actual physician names with credentials. Uses "proof" as a section headline.
- **SkinCheck** (direct competitor): "Take Control of Your Skin Health" — empowerment framing, consumer-first. Their 99% curability stat is a powerful urgency driver worth referencing. Weakness: physician copy is thin and feels tacked on. Opportunity: skinmap owns the physician-first lane entirely.
- **Biobeat**: Leads with the clinical category label ("CUFFLESS 24-HOUR BLOOD PRESSURE MONITORING") before any benefit — signals extreme specificity and clinical credibility. FDA K-numbers as trust signals are powerful once cleared.
- **Mainstay Medical**: "First & only" is the strongest positioning phrase in medical devices. skinmap should own the equivalent: "The only iPhone app that tracks patient skin health longitudinally across visits."
- **Owlet**: FDA clearance in the headline itself. A model for how skinmap should use "IRB-validated" and "FDA pathway" — as primary descriptors, not footnotes.
