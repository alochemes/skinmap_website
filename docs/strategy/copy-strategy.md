# skinmap Copy Strategy — Ongoing Reference
**Last updated:** 2026-03-15
**Purpose:** Living reference for all copy sessions. Load this file at the start of every copy conversation.

---

## The Core Product Truth

skinmap is a longitudinal skin health tracking tool for physicians. It uses an iPhone to document
a patient's full skin record at every visit and uses AI-assisted analysis to flag changes worth
reviewing. It takes ~90 seconds. No hardware. Medicare reimbursable under CPT 96904.

---

## The Two Audiences

### Primary: Medical Providers
Dermatologists, primary care physicians (PCPs), family medicine doctors.

**What they want:**
- To deliver excellent, thorough care to every patient
- To be efficient — they see 20–40 patients per day
- To protect themselves clinically and legally
- Tools that fit into their existing workflow, not disrupt it
- Evidence before they adopt anything new

**What they won't say out loud:**
- "I can't remember every lesion on every patient — it's humanly impossible at this scale"
- "I'm worried a documentation trail could be used against me in a malpractice suit"

**What they respond to in copy:**
- Peer-to-peer tone — speak like a colleague, not a salesperson
- Specific clinical language (CPT codes, sensitivity/specificity, IRB, standard of care)
- Words: document, record, track, evidence, knowing, complete, certainty
- Confidence-building, not fear-inducing

### Secondary: Patients
Health-conscious adults, people with a family history of skin cancer, cancer-concerned individuals.

**What they want:**
- To know their skin is being monitored thoroughly
- Personalized care — to not feel like a number
- Reassurance that their doctor is on top of changes between visits
- To feel empowered to advocate for their own health

**What they won't say out loud:**
- "I'm not confident my doctor actually remembers my specific skin concerns between visits"
- "I wish my doctor tracked my moles the way my dentist tracks my teeth"

**What they respond to in copy:**
- Language of trust and being remembered
- "Your doctor will know" — reassurance framing
- Words: remember, track, record, personalized, changes, history

---

## The Core Tension (the insight everything is built on)

> Patients want their doctor to give them personalized, longitudinal skin care and be on top
> of every change in their skin health. But doctors see hundreds of patients and do not have
> perfect memories — so it is practically impossible for them to deliver that without a tool.

**skinmap resolves this tension** by giving the physician a complete, timestamped visual record
of every patient's skin at every visit — making the impossible expectation possible.

---

## The Hidden Physician Concern: Malpractice

**The fear:** By creating a digital, timestamped record of every lesion at every visit, if the
physician does not catch a developing issue, a malpractice attorney has a documented trail
to use against them.

**The reframe skinmap must own:**
Documentation isn't the liability — inconsistent or absent documentation is. A complete,
timestamped, AI-flagged record proves the standard of care was met. The physician who used
skinmap has evidence they were actively monitoring, flagging, and responding. The physician
without skinmap has no defense at all.

**Copy rules for this concern:**
- This reframe CANNOT appear in the hero or general marketing copy without legal review
- It belongs in: physician FAQ, sales conversations, demo scripts, clinical trust section
- It CAN inform tone: words like "document," "record," "evidence," "knowing," and
  "standard" quietly signal protection without making a legal claim
- Never position documentation as a liability — always as clinical certainty and protection

---

## Hero Subheadline: Principles

**Format:** 2 sentences. Keep them short — aim for under 12 words each.

**Emotional targets:** Curiosity + Relief (primary). Confidence (secondary).

**Dual-audience rule:** Write for the physician first. If a patient reads it, they should
feel reassured that their doctor now has this capability. The copy should work for both
without explicitly addressing both.

**Never do:**
- Imply the physician is currently failing or missing things (belittling)
- Use "empowers you" — sounds like HR, not clinical
- Make the physician feel shame — the scale problem is physical, not a competence failure
- Directly reference malpractice or legal protection in public-facing hero copy

**The documentation frame:**
Words like "record," "document," "knowing exactly," "complete," and "visit by visit"
quietly signal clinical protection and thoroughness without making a legal or FDA claim.

## Current Best Hero Subheadline Candidates (A/B test)

### Option A — Relief + Documentation (recommended control)
> "Your patients deserve a complete skin record. skinmap builds it, visit by visit."

- Doctor reads: this is the tool that fulfills my duty to my patients
- Patient reads: my skin history will be completely documented
- Emotional register: relief + duty
- Documentation signal: "complete skin record," "visit by visit"

### Option B — Confidence + Clinical Certainty
> "Walk into every exam knowing exactly what changed. skinmap makes that possible."

- Doctor reads: I have clinical certainty and the record to back it up
- Patient reads: my doctor will always walk in fully prepared
- Emotional register: confidence
- Documentation signal: "knowing exactly," "changed"

**Recommendation:** A/B test Option A (control) vs Option B. If conversion is lower than
expected on A, flip to B. Option A speaks to duty; Option B speaks to capability.

## Copy Directions Explored (with verdicts)

| Direction | Example | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Relief — trust fulfillment | "Patients come back trusting you to know what changed. skinmap gives you that record." | Strong — keep in reserve |
| Curiosity — scale | "You see hundreds of patients. skinmap remembers every mole." | Strong — Option C candidate |
| Urgency — gap | "Every visit without a skin record is a gap. skinmap closes it." | Risky — can tip into guilt |
| Confidence — walk in knowing | "Walk into every exam knowing exactly what changed." | Strong — current Option B |
| Dual audience direct | "Your patients want to know what changed. Now you both will." | Rejected — too clever, loses physician specificity |
| Curiosity — question | "What changed since their last visit? Now you'll always have the answer." | Rejected by founder |
| Empowerment | "skinmap empowers you to know what changed." | Rejected — "empowers" too soft/HR |
| Belittling (mistake) | "Your patients notice changes between visits. Now, so will you." | Rejected — implies physician incompetence |

## FDA Copy Rules (always apply)

- No "detect," "diagnose," "identify cancer" — use "flag," "track," "document"
- AI = "AI-assisted" never "AI detects" or "AI diagnoses"
- Stats require qualifier: "in internal testing" or "in our clinical pilot"
- No perpetuity claims: "forever," "always," "never misses"
- No superiority claims over unaided physician without cited study
- "clinical-grade" applies to image capture only — not AI analysis
