### Scoring Notes

**D3 scores highest (26/30).** The reimbursement angle is the most distinctive hero play in the medical AI space — no competitor leads with CPT codes. It immediately signals to any dermatologist or PCP that skinmap is a revenue tool, not just a clinical experiment. FDA safety is clean because a billing code reference is not an efficacy claim. The only weakness is near-zero patient resonance, which is mitigated by the secondary "Ask Your Doctor" CTA button.

**D4 and D6 tie at 25/30.** D4 (AI Precision) is tight and clinically grounded. D6 (Accuracy Numbers) has maximum clinical authority — leading with 90%/90% is bold and evidence-first, exactly the brand posture. D6 scores a 4 (not 5) on FDA Safety because the inline qualifier "in internal testing" is required at every use per Compliance Report Issue 7; any truncation creates a violation risk. D4 avoids this by not citing a raw statistic.

**D1 scores 24/30.** The problem-framing approach ("most lesions go undocumented") is resonant with dermatologists and pairs well with either reimbursement or precision angles. Clean synthesis base.

**D7 scores lowest (19/30).** The no-hardware angle is already communicated in the trust strip and How It Works section. Using it as the primary subheadline message undersells the product's clinical value and competitive differentiation.

## Step 2 — Synthesis: 3 Final Versions

### Constraint Verification

**Critical rule:** 2 sentences per subheadline, each sentence ≤8 words. All sentences counted independently before finalization. Counts shown inline.

### Version A — "The Revenue Angle"
*Core direction: D3 (Reimbursement) + D1 problem frame*

**Headline:** Every Mole. Every Patient. Every Visit.

**Subheadline (2 sentences, both ≤8 words):**
- S1: Most lesions go undocumented between visits.
- S2: skinmap supports CPT 96904 reimbursement, no hardware.

**Independent word count:**
- S1: Most(1) lesions(2) go(3) undocumented(4) between(5) visits(6) = **6 words** ✓
- S2: skinmap(1) supports(2) CPT(3) 96904(4) reimbursement(5) no(6) hardware(7) = **7 words** ✓

**Trust strip:** CPT 96904 · HIPAA Compliant · iPhone Only

**Physician CTA:** Request a Demo
**Patient CTA:** Ask Your Doctor →

**Why it works:** No competitor hero leads with a billing code. It immediately qualifies physician visitors — those who care about reimbursable workflow tools read on; those who were unlikely to convert self-select out. The D1 problem frame in S1 gives clinical context before the ROI hook lands in S2. Clean self-contained two-beat rhythm.

**Risk:** Patient resonance is near zero. Entirely mitigated by the "Ask Your Doctor" secondary CTA button, which gives patients a path.

---

### Version B — "The Precision Angle"
*Core direction: D4 (AI Precision) + D6 (Accuracy Numbers)*

**Headline:** See What the Eye Might Miss

**Subheadline (2 sentences, both ≤8 words):**
- S1: Skin changes. Written notes don't capture them.
- S2: 90% sensitivity. 90% specificity. In internal testing.

**Independent word count:**
- S1: Skin(1) changes(2) Written(3) notes(4) don't(5) capture(6) them(7) = **7 words** ✓
- S2: 90%(1) sensitivity(2) 90%(3) specificity(4) In(5) internal(6) testing(7) = **7 words** ✓

**Trust strip:** IRB-Approved Study · 50,000+ Clinical Images · AI-Assisted

**Physician CTA:** Request a Demo
**Patient CTA:** Ask Your Doctor →

**Why it works:** Maximum clinical authority. The headline is drawn directly from the approved skinmap headline bank. S1 frames the clinical gap in 7 words; S2 delivers the evidence in 7 words. This is the strongest version for hospital procurement reviewers and clinical partners who need to see rigor before they schedule a demo.

**FDA note (mandatory):** The "90% sensitivity / 90% specificity" stat carries the required inline qualifier "in internal testing" in S2 exactly as mandated by Compliance Report Issue 7. This qualifier must not be removed or truncated for any layout reason. If the trust strip needs to carry the stat separately, use the full qualifier: "90% sensitivity and 90% specificity in internal validation testing on 50,000+ annotated clinical images."

**Risk:** Less immediate CTA pull for a physician who came to the site to evaluate cost or workflow fit. Those visitors are served by the How It Works and Comparison sections below the fold.

**Physician CTA:** Request a Demo

**Why it works:** Best execution of the dual-audience strategy from CLAUDE.md. S1 speaks to the physician's clinical gap; S2 speaks to patient demand pressure. **Risk:** Lower clinical authority than Version B. S2 is patient-motivational rather than evidence-based, which may read as soft to a physician evaluating clinical rigor. Best suited if analytics show a meaningful volume of patient-origin traffic.

## Step 3 — Recommendation

**Primary recommendation: Version A** for dermatologist and PCP demo conversion. The CPT code in the hero is genuinely distinctive — no competitor in the medical AI category uses it at headline position. If the product's 90-day goal is physician demo requests, Version A drives the sharpest CTA pull.

**Version B** is the right choice if the primary site traffic is clinical evaluators, hospital procurement teams, or investors — the evidence-first framing signals scientific rigor and earns credibility with that audience before they scroll.

**Version C** should be tested when patient traffic becomes a meaningful share of overall site visits (organic SEO, patient advocacy press, physician referrals to the patient-facing page).
